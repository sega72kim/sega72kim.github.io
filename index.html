DEVELOPMENT OF THE IT SPHERE AND ITS INFLUENCE

Welcome to the realm where technology not only shapes our lives but also significantly impacts the environment. The IT sphere, constantly evolving and transforming the world around us, carries both positive and negative consequences for ecology. We stand at a crossroads where innovation meets responsibility for the future of our planet.

Every mouse click, every software update or device upgrade is a step in the world of technology that influences our surrounding environment. Our task is to explore, understand, and discuss the impact of the IT sphere on the environment, outlining both positive perspectives and challenges to overcome.

Our reflections on the influence of IT on the environment will lead us to a genuine understanding of how to effectively harness technology in harmony with nature. Join us on this journey of exploring the opportunities, challenges, and responsibilities that the IT world holds for our planet.

A LITTLE HISTORY

According o Moore's Law, the number of transistors placed on an integrated circuit doubles every 24 months, while the performance of modern processors doubles every 18 months. The viability of this law is direct evidence of the incessantly rapid development of the IT industry in the modern world. Just like any rapidly developing industry, the IT sector has a certain impact on the environment every day, both positively and negatively

Advantages of IT development

1. Development of Environmentally Friendly Technologies
We can note the rapid development of environmentally friendly technologies, replacing past technologies affecting air pollution and other environmental factors. The most obvious examples are electric vehicles; these cars significantly reduce emissions of pollutants and carbon dioxide into the atmosphere, thus significantly helping to preserve the environment. Without the development of the IT industry, the emergence of electric cars would have been practically impossible due to the absence of smart algorithms and programs for their operation.
3. Remote work and reduced commuting
The third significant advantage of IT industry development is the ability for many employees to work remotely. Remote work significantly reduces commuting; working from home reduces the need for constant trips to work, thereby reducing emissions of pollutants and traffic congestion. Remote work also saves energy consumed by giant offices and reduces the consumption of paper resources.
2. Enhanced Process Efficiency
The advancement of information technology has led to a significant enhancement in the efficiency of processes across various spheres of our lives. Automation, digitization, and the use of smart algorithms have optimized workflows, improved service quality, and increased productivity. One of the key advantages of such development is the ability to more efficiently utilize resources while preserving the environment.

Disadvantages of IT development

2. Component production
The second no less significant problem is the production of components. The manufacture of computers, smartphones, and other devices requires resource extraction, which can lead to ecosystem destruction, soil degradation, and the release of toxic substances. The production of many electronic devices requires rare materials such as lithium for batteries or rare earth metals for electronics. Their extraction and processing are often associated with environmental issues, including soil and water pollution.
1. Energy consumption of server centers
The first major problem that we must note is the high energy consumption of server centers. Large server centers consume an enormous amount of energy to maintain operation, leading to the emission of carbon dioxide and other pollutants. Most modern technological innovations require powerful servers and data centers that consume enormous amounts of energy. The cooling processes of these centers also require additional resources. This leads to high energy consumption and emissions of greenhouse gases, negatively affecting the climate.
3. Increasing amount of electronic waste
The final drawback of IT industry development is the increasing amount of electronic waste. The rapid cycle of updating and replacing electronics leads to an increase in electronic waste, which often is not properly disposed of, creating environmental problems. In addition the rapid advancement of technology leads to the emergence of obsolete equipment. Waste from computers, mobile devices, and other electronic gadgets becomes a significant issue. Not all of these devices are properly recycled, which can result in the release of toxic substances into the environment. Components such as lead and cadmium can negatively impact water and soil resources.

Solutions for problems

1. Improvement of modern technologies
Strengthening and implementing modern technologies play a pivotal role in addressing environmental issues. The effective utilization of IT innovations, energy-efficient technologies, smart cities, and digital educational resources reduces the adverse impact on the environment. Promoting energy efficiency, fostering sustainable production methods, and utilizing digital platforms for environmental education are crucial. These advancements in technology not only reduce the negative impact on the environment but also contribute to creating a more sustainable and healthier future for all.
2. Environmentally friendly materials
The development of eco-friendly materials for the IT industry will address environmental issues significantly. Utilizing materials based on biodegradable components or renewable resources reduces the adverse impact on the environment. These materials possess low toxicity levels and can be disposed of without significant harm to ecosystems. Eco-friendly materials also contribute to waste reduction, enhance production sustainability, thereby diminishing the environmental footprint of the IT sector and preserving nature for future generations.
3. Encourage IT companies
Involving IT companies in addressing environmental issues is beneficial for ecology for several reasons. Firstly, the IT industry has the capability to develop innovative technologies for monitoring and managing the environment, effectively tracking and reducing adverse impacts on ecosystems. Secondly, many IT companies strive for energy efficiency in their data centers and infrastructure, reducing energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions. Thirdly, the IT industry supports the development of innovative eco-friendly solutions, including the use of biodegradable materials and waste management technologies. All these aspects enhance companies' sustainability towards environmental challenges and contribute to preserving nature.
